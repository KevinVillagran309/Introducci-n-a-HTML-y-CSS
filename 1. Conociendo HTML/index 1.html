The Brown Bear

About Brown Bears

El oso pardo (Ursus arctos) es originario de partes del norte de Eurasia y América del Norte. Su estado de conservación es actualmente de Preocupación Menor. Hay muchas subespecies dentro de la especie de oso pardo, incluido el oso del Atlas y el oso pardo del Himalaya.

Species

Features

Los osos pardos no siempre son completamente pardos. Algunas pueden ser rojizas o amarillentas. Tienen garras muy grandes y curvas y patas enormes. Los osos pardos machos suelen ser un 30% más grandes que las hembras. Pueden oscilar entre 5 y 9 pies de pies a cabeza.

The Brown Bear

About Brown Bears

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia and North America. Its conservation status is currently Least Concern. There are many subspecies within the brown bear species, including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Species

Features

Brown bears are not always completely brown. Some can be reddish or yellowish. They have very large, curved claws and huge paws. Male brown bears are often 30% larger than female brown bears. They can range from 5 feet to 9 feet from head to toe.

Habitat

Countries with Large Brown Bear Populations

Countries with Small Brown Bear Populations

Some countries with smaller brown bear populations include Armenia, Belarus, Bulgaria, China, Finland, France, Greece, India, Japan, Nepal, Poland, Romania, Slovenia, Turkmenistan, and Uzbekistan.
